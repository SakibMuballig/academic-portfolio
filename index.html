Research

Research

Marine science research focused on satellite-based oceanographic analysis in the Bay of Bengal

Research Work


Variability of Chlorophyll-a in Response to ENSO and IOD Events in the Northern Bay of Bengal: Insights into Spatial and Temporal Dynamics

Undergraduate Thesis  |  Shahjalal University of Science and Technology, 2023

Analyzed 16-year (2003–2018) MODIS-Aqua satellite data to examine chlorophyll-a (Chl-a) variability across monsoon seasons (pre-monsoon, SW monsoon, NE monsoon) in the northern Bay of Bengal. Classified Chl-a fluctuations according to El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) and Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD) phases. Revealed how large-scale climate events drive phytoplankton dynamics and marine productivity in the region.

MODIS-Aqua Google Earth Engine Chlorophyll-a ENSO IOD Bay of Bengal GAM Modelling

View Report

Determination of Phytoplankton Size Class from Remote Sensing Data in the Bay of Bengal

Research Project  |  Shahjalal University of Science and Technology

Used satellite-based ocean color remote sensing to classify phytoplankton size classes — micro-, nano-, and picoplankton — by estimating chlorophyll-a concentrations. Adapted an existing model from Indian Ocean parameters to Bay of Bengal conditions to improve accuracy of size-class estimation. Contributed to understanding of marine ecosystem health and primary productivity monitoring.
